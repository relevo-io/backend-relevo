# Module 2: Searching for Opportunities

Finding the right business is one of the most important stages of the acquisition process. A well-planned search helps identify promising opportunities and avoids wasting time on businesses that do not match your objectives.

## Where to Find Opportunities?

There are several sources available for finding businesses for sale:

- Business acquisition marketplaces.
- Professional networks and personal contacts.
- Chambers of commerce and business associations.
- Financial advisors and brokers.
- Companies facing succession challenges.

## Evaluating Initial Opportunities

During the early stages, it is important to gather general information about each business.

Relevant aspects include:

- Industry sector.
- Location.
- Years in operation.
- Approximate revenue.
- Number of employees.
- Reason for the sale.

## Positive Indicators

Some signs may suggest a promising opportunity:

- Stable revenue.
- Consistent profitability.
- Loyal customer base.
- Strong reputation.
- Growth potential.

## Warning Signs

It is equally important to identify potential risks:

- Excessive dependence on a single customer.
- Continuous decline in sales.
- Known legal issues.
- High employee turnover.
- Insufficient or contradictory information.

> **Tip:** At this stage, the goal is not to make a final decision but to identify which opportunities deserve a more detailed evaluation.

## Conclusion

A structured search based on objective criteria allows buyers to focus on the most promising opportunities and increases the likelihood of a successful acquisition.
