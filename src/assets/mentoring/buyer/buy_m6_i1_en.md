# Module 6: Formalizing the Acquisition

After completing the analysis and negotiation stages, it is time to formalize the acquisition. This phase is critical because it allows the buyer to verify information and ensure that all agreed conditions are properly documented.

## Reviewing Legal Documentation

Before signing any agreement, it is important to carefully review all relevant documentation.

Common documents include:

- Company incorporation documents.
- Corporate bylaws.
- Active contracts.
- Licenses and permits.
- Intellectual property documentation.

The goal is to confirm that no significant hidden risks exist.

## Understanding Due Diligence

Due Diligence is an investigation process used to validate the information provided by the seller.

It typically includes:

- Financial review.
- Legal review.
- Tax review.
- Employment review.
- Operational review.

This process helps identify potential issues before closing the transaction.

## Validating Agreed Terms

Before finalizing the acquisition, it is important to ensure that all negotiated conditions have been properly documented.

Examples include:

- Final purchase price.
- Payment terms.
- Assets included.
- Warranties.
- Future obligations.

## Signing the Contracts

The signing process legally formalizes the acquisition.

Contracts generally define:

- Scope of the transaction.
- Financial terms.
- Responsibilities of each party.
- Agreed warranties.
- Procedures for resolving disputes.

Professional legal support is strongly recommended.

## Preparing for Day One as the New Owner

Once the acquisition is completed, attention should shift toward the transition process.

Important considerations include:

- Communication with employees.
- Communication with customers and suppliers.
- Transfer of systems and access rights.
- Review of internal processes.
- Definition of initial priorities.

> **Tip:** A properly structured acquisition reduces future risks and provides a solid foundation for the next stage of business ownership.

## Conclusion

Properly formalizing the acquisition is essential for ensuring a secure transition and protecting the buyer’s interests during the first stages of managing the business.
