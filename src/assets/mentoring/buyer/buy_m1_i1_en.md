# Module 1: Defining Your Acquisition Goals

Buying a business is a strategic decision that requires careful planning and analysis. Before starting the search for opportunities, it is essential to clearly define the objectives of the acquisition in order to focus on businesses that truly match the buyer’s expectations.

## Why Buy a Business?

There are several reasons why someone may decide to acquire a business:

- Start an entrepreneurial activity with an already established foundation.
- Diversify investments.
- Expand an existing company.
- Access new markets or customers.
- Acquire specialized knowledge, technology, or talent.
- Create a new source of income.

Understanding the main motivation behind the acquisition will help guide decisions throughout the process.

## Define Your Available Budget

Before evaluating opportunities, it is important to determine your actual financial capacity.

Some key aspects to consider include:

- Available personal capital.
- Access to external financing.
- Transaction-related costs.
- Resources required for future growth.
- Emergency reserves for unexpected situations.

Having a clearly defined budget will help narrow down potential opportunities.

## Select Industries of Interest

Not every business is suitable for every buyer. It is advisable to identify industries that align with your interests or areas of expertise.

Useful evaluation criteria include:

- Industry knowledge and experience.
- Growth trends.
- Competitive environment.
- Barriers to entry.
- Profitability potential.

## Define Preferred Locations

Location can significantly impact the management and profitability of a business.

Consider factors such as:

- Geographic proximity.
- Customer availability.
- Access to suppliers.
- Operating costs.
- Growth opportunities in the area.

## Establish Search Criteria

Clearly defining search criteria from the beginning will make it easier to identify suitable opportunities.

For example:

- Minimum and maximum revenue.
- Number of employees.
- Industry sector.
- Location.
- Business age.
- Expected profitability level.

> Tip: The more clearly you define your acquisition goals, the easier it will be to eliminate unsuitable opportunities and focus on those that truly create value.

## Conclusion

Clearly defining your acquisition objectives is the first step toward a successful purchase. A solid understanding of your budget, target industry, and desired business characteristics will enable a more efficient search process and more confident decision-making.
