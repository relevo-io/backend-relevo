# Module 3: Valuing the Business

Determining the value of a business is one of the most important steps before starting a sale process. An appropriate valuation helps establish realistic expectations and supports negotiations with potential buyers.

## Why Is a Proper Valuation Important?

Setting the price too high may reduce buyer interest and extend the sale process. On the other hand, setting it too low may result in a loss of value for the owner.

A well-supported valuation helps to:

- Build trust.
- Justify the asking price.
- Facilitate negotiations.
- Attract qualified buyers.

## Factors That Influence Value

A company’s value depends on many different factors:

- Annual revenue.
- Profitability.
- Growth history.
- Market position.
- Customer diversification.
- Dependence on the owner.
- Financial condition.

No single factor determines the final value.

## The Importance of Profitability

Buyers usually pay close attention to the business’s ability to generate recurring profits.

Common indicators include:

- Net profit.
- Operating margin.
- Cash flow.
- Performance trends.

A profitable company is generally more attractive to investors.

## Company Assets and Resources

In addition to financial performance, value may also come from:

- Machinery and equipment.
- Real estate.
- Registered trademarks.
- Proprietary technology.
- Customer portfolio.
- Strategic contracts.

These elements can increase the perceived value of the business.

## Growth Potential

Many buyers evaluate not only the current situation but also future opportunities.

Commonly assessed aspects include:

- New markets.
- New products or services.
- Competitive advantages.
- Expansion opportunities.

> **Tip:** Buyers do not only pay for what a business is today, but also for what it can become in the future.

## Conclusion

A realistic valuation based on objective information helps streamline the sales process and creates a stronger foundation for negotiations.
