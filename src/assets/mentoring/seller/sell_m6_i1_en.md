# Module 6: Closing the Sale

After negotiations are completed, one of the most important stages of the process begins: closing the transaction. This phase includes final verifications, formalizing agreements, and preparing the transition to the new owner.

## Final Documentation Review

Before signing any agreement, it is important to ensure that all documentation is accurate, complete, and consistent with the information shared throughout the process.

It is advisable to review:

- Annual financial statements.
- Active contracts.
- Licenses and permits.
- Employment-related information.
- Outstanding financial obligations.

A thorough review helps prevent last-minute issues.

## Due Diligence

It is common for the buyer to conduct a detailed review of the business before completing the acquisition.

This review may include:

- Financial matters.
- Legal matters.
- Tax matters.
- Operational aspects.
- Commercial performance.

Transparency during this stage is essential for maintaining trust.

## Negotiating Final Details

Even at this stage, certain conditions may still require clarification or adjustment.

Examples include:

- Payment structure.
- Transaction timeline.
- Assets included in the sale.
- Agreed warranties.
- Temporary involvement of the seller.

All agreements should be clearly documented.

## Signing the Contracts

Once a final agreement has been reached, the transaction is formally documented through legal contracts.

These contracts typically define:

- Scope of the transaction.
- Purchase price.
- Payment terms.
- Rights and obligations of each party.
- Warranties and liabilities.

Professional legal advice is highly recommended.

## Preparing the Transition

A well-planned transition helps ensure business continuity after the acquisition.

This may include:

- Knowledge transfer.
- Introduction to customers.
- Introduction to suppliers.
- Temporary support from the former owner.
- Transfer of documentation and system access.

> **Tip:** Closing the sale is not the end of the process. A smooth transition helps preserve business value and supports a positive relationship between both parties.

## Conclusion

Closing a business sale involves much more than signing a contract. Proper management of the final stages reduces risks and facilitates an orderly transfer of ownership.
